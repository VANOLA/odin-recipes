Lentil Soup

This soup is a vegetarian lentil soup. I decided to use french green lentils because they are particularly suited for soups and stews. This soup feels like a big bowl of comfort and is perfect for a fall day.

The secret ingredient in this soup is a parmesan rind! It adds a perfect, subtle complexity that is often missing in soups. However, feel free to omit if necessary. You may also substitute the rind of any other hard cheese such as asiago, or parmigiano reggiano. Now you have a reason to save the rinds of your parmesan cheese wedges! They freeze perfectly well. I hope you enjoy this soup.

Ingredients

  • 2 T olive oil
  • 1 small diced onion
  • salt & pepper to taste
  • 3 medium carrots, diced
  • 3 stalks celery, diced
  • 2 medium sweet potatos, or red potatos
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1.5 cups green (French) lentils
  • 28oz can crushed tomatoes
  • 6 cups of vegetable stock, storebought is fine
  • 1 parmesan cheese rind

Preparation

  1. In a dutch oven over medium heat, heat up the olive oil. Add the onions with the salt and pepper, and saute until the onions are transluscent and fragrant, about 4-5 minutes.
  2. Add the carrots, celery, and potatoes. Saute for another 8-10 minutes, or until tender. Add the garlic, cumin, and thyme, and saute for another 30 seconds. Stir continuously to avoid burning the garlic.
  3. Add the lentils, and deglaze the pan with crushed tomatoes and vegetable stock. Stir in the parmesan cheese rind if using, and simmer soup for 30 minutes, or until lentils are tender. Adjust salt and pepper if necessary.
  4. Enjoy alone or with crusty bread!
